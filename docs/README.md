# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Docs. This documentation provides living guides and checklists for all aspects of project management within OctoAcme, helping teams collaborate, onboard, and deliver using proven, repeatable methods.

## Overview of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme's project management approach follows a lightweight, end-to-end lifecycle designed for iterative delivery and clear ownership. Work typically starts with **Initiation**, where teams validate the business need, identify stakeholders, define success metrics, outline an initial timeline, and capture early risks. The primary output is a **Project One-pager/Charter** (problem, goal, success metrics), plus a stakeholder communication plan and a go/no-go decision to proceed into planning.

During **Planning**, OctoAcme turns approved initiatives into an actionable backlog and release path. Teams hold a kickoff, break work into shippable increments, add acceptance criteria to backlog items, estimate scope, define a **Definition of Done**, and identify dependencies and integration points. Risks and cross-team dependencies are tracked explicitly (e.g., in a risk register and on the project board) and escalated via a defined path when they threaten delivery.

**Execution** centers on consistent workflows and team rhythm. Work is managed on a project board with clear stages (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done) and a PR process that emphasizes small changes, linked issues and acceptance criteria, automated checks, and peer review before merge. The team cadence includes daily standups to surface blockers, weekly delivery syncs to review progress and risks, and sprint/milestone demos to keep stakeholders aligned.

Roles and communication norms are defined to reduce ambiguity: **Project Managers** coordinate delivery, schedules, risks, and stakeholder updates; **Product Managers** own outcomes, prioritization, and success measurement; **Developers** implement and test, contribute to estimation, and participate in reviews; **QA/Testing** validates against acceptance criteria; and **Stakeholders** provide input and approvals. Quality assurance is built in through unit/integration testing, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, CI-based lint/security scanning, and manual QA when needed—especially ahead of releases. Releases use standardized checklists (acceptance criteria met, CI green, release notes, rollback plan, staged smoke tests, post-deploy verification) and, if something goes wrong, teams follow an incident/rollback playbook with a blameless retrospective to drive continuous improvement.
